# CivicEdge ERP - Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)
## 1. Introduction
CivicEdge ERP is a comprehensive solution specifically designed for Government Bodies and Non-Profit Organizations (NGOs). This SOP outlines the end-to-end customer journey, detailing how to utilize the system for tracking funds, executing programs, managing donors, staying compliant, and measuring impact.
## 2. Core Modules Overview
### 2.1 Governance & Compliance
*   **Board Member & Meeting**: Track board members and organize board meetings with recorded minutes.
*   **Compliance Tracker**: Track deadlines for regulatory compliances.
*   **Utilization Certificate**: Generate and manage utilization certificates for funds used.
*   **FCRA Registration & Return**: Specifically manage Foreign Contribution Regulation Act compliance and annual returns.
### 2.2 Fund & Budget Management
*   **Fund**: Create and manage specific funds (e.g., General Fund, Restricted Fund).
*   **Budget Head**: Establish budgetary categories to allocate spending appropriately.
*   **Fund Transfer & Transaction**: Log individual transactions and move funds across different accounts or budgets.
### 2.3 Donor & Grant Management
*   **Donor & Pledge**: Maintain a CRM of donors and register their pledged amounts.
*   **Donation & Receipt**: Record incoming donations and issue automated, formatted donation receipts.
*   **Grant & Grant Application**: Apply for grants, track approval status, and manage the full grant lifecycle.
*   **Grant Disbursement & Utilization**: Record funds disbursed by grantors and track how those funds are utilized over time.
### 2.4 Programs & Beneficiary Management
*   **Program & Program Activity**: Define social or operational programs and the specific activities under them.
*   **Beneficiary & Enrollment**: Maintain a registry of beneficiaries and enroll them in specific programs.
### 2.5 Volunteer Management
*   **Volunteer**: Register volunteers along with their skills and availability.
*   **Volunteer Assignment**: Assign volunteers to specific Program Activities.
*   **Volunteer Timesheet**: Log the hours volunteers spend on assigned activities.
### 2.6 Procurement & HR
*   **Staff**: Maintain a roster of core staff members.
*   **Procurement Request & Tender**: Raise requests for materials/services and manage the tendering process.

## 3. End-to-End User Journey (Customer POV)
### Phase 1: Setup and Compliance
1.  **System Initialization**: The administrator sets up core profiles including **Staff** and **Board Members**.
2.  **Compliance Setup**: All legal deadlines (like tax filings, NGO renewals) are added to the **Compliance Tracker**. If receiving foreign funds, the **FCRA Registration** details are entered.
3.  **Budget Structuring**: Finance managers create the required **Budget Heads** and initial **Funds** to mirror the organization’s accounting structure.
### Phase 2: Funding and Budgeting
1.  **Donation Collection**: As donors express interest, they are entered into the **Donor** module. Commitments are logged as **Pledges**. Once the money is received, a **Donation** entry is created, and a **Donation Receipt** is instantly generated and sent.
2.  **Grant Acquisition**: The organization files a **Grant Application**. Once approved, it becomes an active **Grant**, and incoming tranches are recorded via **Grant Disbursement**.
3.  **CSR Tracking**: For corporate funds, specific **CSR Projects** are created to keep these funds ring-fenced and easily reportable.
### Phase 3: Program Execution
1.  **Program Creation**: Program managers define the overarching **Program** (e.g., "Rural Education Initiative") and break it down into **Program Activities** (e.g., "School Supply Distribution").
2.  **Beneficiary Enrollment**: Eligible individuals are added as **Beneficiaries** and officially enrolled into the program via **Beneficiary Enrollment**.
3.  **Resource Allocation**:
    *   **Volunteers** are onboarded, assigned to activities (**Volunteer Assignment**), and their hours are tracked (**Volunteer Timesheet**).
    *   Materials needed for the program are requested via **Procurement Request** and, if necessary, a **Tender**.
4.  **Financial Transactions**: All expenses incurred during the program are logged as **Fund Transactions**, mapped back to the specific **Budget Head** and **Fund**.
### Phase 4: Tracking, Reporting & Auditing
1.  **Utilization Reporting**: Finance teams use the **Grant Utilization Report** to prove to donors that funds were spent correctly. They generate **Utilization Certificates** for government or institutional donors.
2.  **Board Reviews**: Progress is presented in scheduled **Board Meetings**. The **Board Meeting Attendance Register** ensures a quorum is documented.
3.  **Annual Returns**: At year-end, the **FCRA Annual Return** is generated directly from the system's tracked foreign contributions, ensuring perfect compliance.

## 4. Key Reports & Dashboards
The system provides out-of-the-box analytical reports to aid decision-making:
*   **Fund Balance Report**: Real-time snapshot of available funds.
*   **Grant Utilization Summary**: High-level view of how much of each grant has been spent vs. remains.
*   **Program-wise Expenditure**: Analyzes which programs consume the most resources.
*   **Volunteer Hours Summary**: Quantifies the value of volunteer efforts.
*   **CSR Utilization Dashboard**: Interactive view for corporate sponsors.
*   **Donor Ledger**: Complete financial history for any given donor.
